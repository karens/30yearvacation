This interesting study by Merrill Lynch and Age Wave examines where retirees choose to live in retirement. Among other interesting results, they found that not only do many retirees choose NOT to downsize their home, they actually move to a larger home to have room for family members to come and visit and as a place that the extended family can gather. That would have a significant impact on planned expenses in retirement that many people probably haven't considered.

BBC News
Two out of five people over the age of 60 do voluntary work, a survey has suggested. A fifth of those questioned helped at least two charities. Women were more likely to support children or lunch clubs while men volunteered at local football clubs or health charities. Almost 700 over-60s were questioned by the Royal Voluntary Service who found that most - 83% - did so because they believed charity work was important. Nearly half of those questioned said they chose to volunteer because they needed to feel their life had a purpose - and 3% said they needed time away from their partners.

About This Site

Retirement sounds great. You quit work, play golf, and travel, and have no responsibilities. Who wouldn't love it? But I could easily spend 30 or more years in retirement, a virtual 30 year "vacation".

Does that idea of retirement still make sense? Can I afford to support myself for 30 years without a pay check? How can anyone plan and save for all the things that might happen over 30 years? When would anyone ever be "ready" to retire? Can I do something meaningful to make it more than a vacation? This site is my attempt to find answers to my questions about my own "30 year vacation", whenever, and if ever, that happens.
