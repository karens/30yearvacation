A more satisfying approach may be 'parallel play.'</p><p>Try watching two children in a sandbox—a boy playing with toy trucks and a girl using a pail and shovel to build sand castles. It's a phenomenon called parallel play—two individuals engrossed in separate but parallel activities. And it might hold the key to happiness in later life. Eight years ago, I wrote a book about the challenges that partners can expect to face when planning for and living together during retirement. In the time since, I have seen thousands of couples in connection with speeches, seminars, therapy sessions and email/mail/phone encounters.
