Annuities and Retirement Happiness

Aug
07
2017
Submitted on Mon, 08/07/2017 - 8:17 am
Willis Towers Watson

An interesting study by Willis Towers Watson determined that having an annuity income source was a significant factor in retirement happiness. An annuity income might be provided by an old-fashioned pension, but many employees no longer have access to that kind of pension. The alternative for them would be to use personal savings or funds from a 401k to purchase an annuity from an insurance company. The study found that the presence of some sort of annuity income stream was a particularly significant factor in retirement happiness in households without a lot of retirement investments.

This report examines the evidence on the cost of conflicted investment advice and its effects on Americans’ retirement savings, focusing on IRAs. Investment losses due to conflicted advice result from the incentives conflicted payments generate for financial advisers to steer savers into products or investment strategies that provide larger payments to the adviser but are not necessarily the best choice for the saver.

About This Site

Retirement sounds great. You quit work, play golf, and travel, and have no responsibilities. Who wouldn't love it? But I could easily spend 30 or more years in retirement, a virtual 30 year "vacation".

Does that idea of retirement still make sense? Can I afford to support myself for 30 years without a pay check? How can anyone plan and save for all the things that might happen over 30 years? When would anyone ever be "ready" to retire? Can I do something meaningful to make it more than a vacation? This site is my attempt to find answers to my questions about my own "30 year vacation", whenever, and if ever, that happens.
